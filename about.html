A Life in Music & Ministry

Born and raised in Memphis, Tennessee, Kirk Whalum grew up immersed in the rich musical heritage of the South. From gospel choirs to jazz clubs, music was always more than a career — it was a calling.

Over four decades, Kirk has built a legacy that spans genres and generations. He's collaborated with legends like Whitney Houston, Luther Vandross, and Larry Carlton. His saxophone work on Whitney Houston's iconic hit has been heard by billions worldwide.

But Kirk's story goes beyond the stage. As an ordained minister with a Masters of Art in Religion, he brings the same spiritual depth to his worship leading as he does to his jazz performances.

The Beginning

Signed first record deal and began touring nationally. Started collaborating with major R&B and pop artists.

Rise to Prominence

Became one of the most sought-after session saxophonists. Performed on Whitney Houston's iconic recordings and toured with major acts worldwide.

Grammy Recognition

Ordained as a minister. Began teaching at Visible Music College. Launched the Bible In Your Ear podcast.
